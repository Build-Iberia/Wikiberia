# Creating and configuring a claim

The official BTE website features a claim system that allows keeping track of the percentage of territory completed and in progress, as well as knowing who built each area.

## Accesing the claims' website

The first step is to go to the Build The Earth website, where you’ll follow all the steps to create your claim. It’s important that you can log in to your Discord account via your browser.

`VIDEO WEBP 1`

1. Go to the official Build The Earth website (you can click here).
2. In the upper right corner, click on the Login button to log into the website with your Discord account.

  > **If you are already logged in:** skip to step 4..

3. The website will redirect you to the Discord login process. On the Discord authorization screen, scroll to the bottom of the permissions list and click the **`Authorize`** button.

  > **You need to be logged into Discord:** if you haven't logged into Discord from your browser, it will prompt you to log in with your Discord account.

4. The page will bring you back to the main screen of the official Build The Earth website. Once there, click on the upper right corner where your username appears and access the "Claim Map" option (you can also access it directly from **[here](https://buildtheearth.net/map/edit)**).

## Creating a new claim

Within this page, we can view claims from any part of the world on a map.

Creating a claim consists of making a selection which includes the building or area of interest and adding the required information.

`VIDEO WEBP 2`

1. With your mouse cursor on the map, navigate to the area where you want to make a claim.
  > To zoom: use the mouse scroll wheel or the controls located on the right side of the map.

  > To pan vertically and horizontally: click and hold while moving the mouse.

2. In the side menu (left), scroll to the bottom and press the **`Create new Claim`** button.
3. Move to the first corner of the building or area you want to claim and click on it. You will see a yellow circle appear where you placed the first point.
4. Repeat this step with all the vertices that delimit the area or building until you reach the last vertex.
5. On the last vertex, double-click to place the final point and close the selection. You will see the selection change from yellow to blue.


## Adding claim information

Once the claim selection is closed, some fields will appear on the left side to be filled in with information about the claim.

`VIDEO WEBP 3`

* In **"Team":** Select the build team corresponding to the claim (Build Iberia). Click inside the field to expand the options and select the one that fits your claim.
* In **"Claim Name":** give the claim a name. This can be the name of the building, area, street, etc. Whatever you consider correct so that people understand what it is and what area the claim covers.
* In **"City":** Fill in the name of the town/city to which the claim belongs. The claim system will automatically generate a name based on the geographical location, but it is recommended to fill in this field.
* In **"Description":** You can add any additional information you consider necessary about the claim. This can include geographical, demographic, or historical information, etc.
* If the construction or area you claimed is complete: check the **"Claim is finished"** option. You can always create a claim while you are in the process of building an area and then return to the claim later to edit the info and check this box once it's done.
* It is important that the **"Show on map"** option is active, as this is what allows your claim and all its information to be displayed on the map for everyone.
* In **"Builders":** you can add all the people who have helped or collaborated with you to complete the build (if applicable). You have to write the Discord account of the user you want to add to the claim.

2. Finally, to save all the information, press the **`Create`** button and wait until you receive a confirmation in the bottom right corner of the screen indicating that your claim has been successfully created.
