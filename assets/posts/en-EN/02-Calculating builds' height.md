# Calculating builds' height

Another important step when building in Iberia is finding out the height of the building you want to construct. To do this, we will explain 3 different methods.

## With Google Earth Pro

In areas where there is a 3D model of the terrain, you can use Google Earth Pro to obtain the heights.

`VIDEO WEBP 1`

### Elevation difference

1. Open Google Earth Pro and search for the location of the building of interest.
2. Place your cursor at the bottom of the building, at ground level (sidewalk).
3. In the bottom-right corner, the elevation above sea level for that point will appear (shown as **"elev."**). Note it down for later.
4. Move your cursor to the top of the building and note down the elevation above sea level again.
5. Subtract the bottom elevation from the top elevation to get the total height of the building.

### With the ruler tool

1. At the top of the screen, select the ruler icon.
2. Select the **"3D Path"** option.
3. Make sure the unit of measurement is set to meters.
4. Left-click on the two points between which you want to find the distance and you will get the measurement.

  > Tip: Try to make the line as straight as possible, as any deviation will make the measured distance greater than the real one .

5. Remember to clear the previous measurement before making a new one.

## With Google Maps

In case the area you are building does not have a 3D model, you can use a Google Maps extension developed by Build The Earth (download **[here](https://discord.com/channels/690908396404080650/1270280302178205758)**)

`VIDEO WEBP 2`

1. Open Google Maps and search for the location of the building whose height you want to measure.
2. Enter Street View (360° view) in front of the building.
3. In the top-right corner, expand the extension menu.
4. A red crosshair will appear in the center of the screen. Place the crosshair at the lowest part of the building you want to measure.
5. On the right side, in the extension menu, press the **`Point 1`** button to mark the bottom position of the building.
6. Move the camera toward the top of the same building, trying to follow the building's vertical line, and press the **`Point 2`** button, then check the **`Height(m)`** box.
7. The height of the building will appear in the field below **`Height(m)`**. Since it displays in decimals, you will need to make approximations. We recommend rounding up from 0.65.
